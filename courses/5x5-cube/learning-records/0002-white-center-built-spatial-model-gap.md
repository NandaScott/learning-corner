# White center built; spatial model of insertions is the gap

Learner completed the white center unaided after L2 (evidence: reported done mid-drill). The blocker is conceptual, not manual: tracking *which piece goes where* during wide-move insertions on a full-color cube overloads them.

**Implications:** Free-build skills are fine — don't re-teach bar assembly. Piece-focused visuals are the fix, and the learner found the winning form themselves: the 3D player with `L2C` stickering (edges/corners blanked, centers only). SVG storyboards were tried and rejected as not helpful. For remaining maneuvers, use a noise-stripped 3D player view; L2C works through the centers stage but hides edges, so an edge-focused view is needed from pairing onward. Expect the same conceptualization question at L3's harder inserts; budget lesson space for it.
