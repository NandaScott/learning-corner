Interleaved review

Re-reading feels like learning but mostly builds fluency — the illusion of mastery. Retrieving from memory, cold, with topics shuffled, is what builds storage strength. That's the whole point of this page.

How to use this

Questions are drawn from all five lessons and shuffled every run — by design. Interleaving (mixing topics rather than blocking them) is harder than reviewing one lesson at a time, and that difficulty is exactly what makes it stick.1 Answer from memory before reading the feedback. Miss one? The feedback names the lesson to revisit. Then hit Shuffle & retry and run it again in a few days — that spacing is the second half of durable memory.

Ask your teacher. Missed the same lesson twice? Tell me which — I'll re-explain it a different way, or drill just that concept. And when you can clear this cold two sessions apart, you're interview-ready on the fundamentals.
