- **L1 — Tempo and initiative.** Initiative = who sets the problem; tempo = holding it over time. Four steps: (1) initiative is about who reacts to whom; (2) action economy — an activation is a resource, removal-before-activation is a double swing; (3) forcing reactions — spend their turn on your threats; (4) seizing it — alpha strike vs bait-and-punish. Synthesis: being ahead means pressing tempo (P1 no-coasting made active); tempo is the meta-skill that recruits P3/P4/P5.

## Verification done

- All Sun Tzu quotes verified verbatim against the Giles translation (MIT Internet Classics Archive): "The clever combatant imposes his will on the enemy, but does not allow the enemy's will to be imposed on him" (Ch VI); "Appear at points which the enemy must hasten to defend; march swiftly to places where you are not expected" (Ch VI); "Whoever is first in the field and awaits the coming of the enemy, will be fresh for the fight; whoever is second in the field and has to hasten to battle will arrive exhausted" (Ch VI).
- 40K structure verified against `wh40k11ed`: game is **I-go-you-go** at the turn level; a turn runs Command → Movement → Shooting → Charge → Fight; the **Fight phase alternates unit selection**. So "kill it before it acts" is most literal in the Fight phase; elsewhere it is "kill on your turn → zero output on their following turn." This distinction is flagged in the lesson (the reference doc's generic "alternating-turn / activations" language corrected to the actual 40K model).
