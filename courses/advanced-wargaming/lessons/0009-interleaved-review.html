Interleaved review

Re-reading the lessons builds fluency — the feeling of knowing. Retrieving from memory, cold, with the eight advanced principles shuffled, builds storage strength. That's what this page is for.

How to use this

Questions are drawn from all eight lessons and shuffled every run. Interleaving the principles rather than reviewing one at a time is harder than blocked practice, and that difficulty is what makes it stick.1 Many questions pit the right answer against a near-miss: the shallow version that sounds right. Answer from memory before reading the feedback, then hit Shuffle & retry, and run it again in a few days — that spacing is the second half of durable memory.

Go deeper. Missed the same principle twice? Flag it — it's worth re-explaining a different way, or running a live board state through it. When you can clear this cold two sessions apart, the advanced layer is yours to reason from, not just recall.
