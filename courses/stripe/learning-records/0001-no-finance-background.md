# Learner has no accounting or finance background

The learner disclosed unfamiliarity with accounting/finance terminology at the start of the course (asked for plain definitions of "acquirer", "issuer", etc. after Lesson 1). Every finance term must be defined in plain language on first use, anchored to everyday experiences (hotel-hold, "the issuer issued the card"); the course glossary was created in response and is written jargon-free.

**Implications:** Lessons can assume engineering fluency (state machines, HMAC, retries needed no scaffolding) but zero payments-industry vocabulary. When later lessons need terms like "merchant of record", "KYC", or "chargeback", introduce them as new vocabulary, not assumed knowledge.
