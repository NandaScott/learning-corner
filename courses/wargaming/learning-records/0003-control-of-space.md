# 0003 — Threat range and the control of space (L3)

**Date:** 2026-07-06
**Status:** current

## What L3 teaches

Principle 3: the atom of board space is the **threat range** (movement + reach). A unit controls its whole threat range whether or not it fires, because a rational opponent must respect the footprint. The board is a map of overlapping threat ranges: yours / theirs / contested. Terrain + line of sight break the symmetry (threaten without being threatened). Because play is turn-based, read one turn **forward** — project each enemy unit its full movement; safe-now is not safe.

Good positioning in one line: maximise the space you threaten, minimise the space that threatens you.

## Ties to prior lessons (backward only)

- **L2:** picks up "board space," which L2 spent as a resource but never defined. Also reuses L2's exchange-rate lens — control is the cheapest buy (a threat range works without firing).
- **L1:** contested space is where objectives are won, so controlling space around a point *is* protecting the scoring.

## Held back for later principles (no forward ref)

- **How much force to bring** to a contested point is concentration (P4) — L3 stops at *where* force meets, not *how much*. The ask-teacher teases exactly this into L4.
- **When to commit / deferral / optionality** is timing (P5). Kept distinct from L3's one-turn-forward *spatial* read, which is a safety projection, not a timing decision. Watch this boundary in L5.
