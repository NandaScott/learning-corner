# 0001 — Baseline and mission

**Date:** 2026-07-06
**Status:** current

## Context

The learner arrived with a self-authored outline (`~/Downloads/five-principles-of-wargaming.md`): five principles of wargaming, a load-bearing chain that orders them, and two cross-cutting supporting skills — framed with Warhammer 40,000 as the concrete game and Sun Tzu's *Art of War* as the timeless parallel. The document is explicitly written as "source material for generating a structured lesson plan."

## Decision — the mission

Confirmed via two questions at kickoff:

- **Win condition of the course: theory-first mastery, anchored to real 40K decisions.** Own the transferable strategic model deeply, but keep every principle nailed to a concrete board state. (Not "play better 40K" — the goal is the model, not table results; not pure abstraction either — 40K keeps it measurable.)
- **40K baseline: knows the rules, plays rarely.** Uses CP / activations / wounds / threat range fluently in the outline. So: **assume the mechanics; supply the concrete board examples** rather than lean on the learner's own recent games.

## Consequences for teaching

- Do **not** re-derive the ideas from scratch or explain basic rules. The job is to **sharpen**: pin the mechanism under each principle, verify the Sun Tzu parallels verbatim, harden the chain, and add the retrieval scaffolding the raw outline lacks.
- Treat the five principles as a **connected model with a dependency order** — Principle 1 (value) is the axiom the other four depend on. Teach it first; make the dependency explicit.
- Every specific tactic is one of the five principles firing in a board state. Teach so specifics become **regenerable, not memorised**.
- Prioritise **storage strength** (recall, spacing, interleaving) over fluency — same as the learner's prior courses.
