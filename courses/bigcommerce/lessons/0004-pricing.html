How BigCommerce Works · Lesson 4

Pricing

Lesson 3 froze a list_price onto a line item without asking where that number came from. This lesson answers it. In BigCommerce a price is not a stored field — it is the output of an ordered stack of overrides: catalog price, sale price, variant price, price lists, customer-group discounts, bulk breaks, modifier adjustments, and finally tax. Understanding the order is understanding the price.

The concept

In a real store, "the price" of a thing is rarely one number. The same product costs less on sale, less again at quantity, maybe less for a wholesale account, more once tax is added, and different amounts in different currencies. A store that stored a single price per product could not express any of that.

So a catalog does not store the final price — it stores the inputs, and computes the price a given shopper sees at a given moment. The design question is only: when two rules both want to set the price, which one wins? That ordering — the priority of overrides — is the whole model.

How BigCommerce handles it

BigCommerce documents this literally, as a "price order of operations": a table "read from top to bottom, with the default price having the lowest weight and the tax having the highest weight when calculating price."1 Each layer can override the ones above it. The full table has sixteen rows; these are the load-bearing ones:

  PRICE ORDER OF OPERATIONS   (top = lowest weight; each layer can override those above)

   1  default product price          ── the catalog floor
   2  product sale_price             ── overrides the default
   3  variant price                  ── overrides the product's sale price
   4  variant sale_price             ── overrides the variant price
   ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   5  PRICE LIST variant price       ── overrides ALL of the above;
                                          also excludes the SKU from product bulk pricing
   6-8  price-list sale / bulk / pick list
   ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  10  product MODIFIER adjuster      ── adds on top (+$5 insurance, +10%)   [Lesson 2]
  11  customer-group discount        ── overrides variant sale price
                                          …but does NOT apply on price-list prices
  12  product bulk pricing           ── quantity breaks; skipped for price-list SKUs
   ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  14  discounts                      ]  cart / promotion layer  [Lessons 5-6]
  15  coupons                        ]
  16  TAX                            ── always last, after shipping and promotions

        the resolved result of running this stack  ==  calculated_price (read-only)

Price is the output of this pipeline, not a field. calculated_price is where the resolved number lands.

① Price is a stack, not a field

The number a shopper pays is computed by walking the layers, each overriding the last. The resolved result is exposed as calculated_price, a read-only field: "the price of the variant as seen on the storefront. This price takes into account sale_price and any price adjustment rules that are applicable to this variant."2 This is the field people misread — see the trap. Everything below is just "which layer sets the price before calculated_price reports it."

② The catalog base — price, sale, variant

The bottom four layers all live on the catalog objects from Lesson 2. A product has a default price; a sale_price overrides it; a variant's own price overrides the product's; a variant sale_price overrides that.1 A variant's price is override-or-inherit: "if this value is null, the product's default price… will be used as the base price."2 So a plain product with one base variant simply uses the product price — the Lesson 2 base-variant rule paying off again.

③ Price lists — per-variant overrides for a channel or group

A price list is "a collection of price records," and "a price record is a price override for a particular variant… at a minimum, it contains a variant ID, a price, and a currency."3 Two facts make price lists the sharp edge of the model. First, they are variant-level only: "the prices are specified exclusively at the variant level. If an active price list doesn't contain prices for a variant, then the catalog pricing will be used."3 Second, they attach to an audience: "you can assign a price list to a specific sales channel, customer group, or customer group on a specific sales channel."3 This is how one product carries a retail price and a wholesale price at once — different price lists for different customer groups.

④ Customer groups and bulk pricing

Two more layers sit above the base. A customer-group discount overrides the variant sale price — but "customer group discounts do not apply to price list prices."1 And product bulk pricing gives quantity breaks (a type of price, percent, or fixed across a quantity_min/quantity_max band),4 but "will not apply for SKUs that have price list prices."1 Notice the pattern: a price-list price doesn't just win its own row — it switches off two later layers for that SKU.

⑤ Modifiers add on top; tax comes last

Lesson 2's modifier adjusters land here as a pricing layer: fixed or percentage amounts "added or removed from the total product price."1 They add on rather than override — a $5 engraving fee stacks onto whatever the base resolved to. Then, after discounts, coupons, and shipping, tax is applied last.1 Tax is where tax-inclusive vs tax-exclusive lives: the resolved price carries an entered_inclusive flag — "determines whether the as_entered price is inclusive or exclusive of tax" — alongside computed tax_inclusive and tax_exclusive values.5

Where it surfaces in the API

The inputs live on catalog objects; a dedicated batch endpoint resolves the whole stack for a given audience.

  • Catalog — /v3/catalog/products and /variants carry price, sale_price, retail_price, cost_price, read-only calculated_price, and bulk_pricing_rules.2
  • Price Lists — /v3/pricelists, records at /v3/pricelists/{id}/records, and assignments at /v3/pricelists/assignments (tying a list to customer_group_id and/or channel_id).3
  • Customer Groups — the v2 Customer Groups API; a group attaches a price list via a discount_rules entry of type price_list.3
  • Pricing API (batch) — POST /v3/pricing/products resolves the full order-of-operations for a channel + currency + customer group, returning calculated_price with tax variants.5
// CATALOG price fields — the inputs (product + one variant), scrubbed
{
  "price": 100.00,
  "sale_price": 79.99,          // overrides price
  "retail_price": 120.00,       // "compare at" / MSRP display only
  "cost_price": 40.00,          // never exposed to the storefront
  "calculated_price": 79.99,    // read-only: the RESOLVED number
  "bulk_pricing_rules": [
    { "quantity_min": 10, "quantity_max": 50, "type": "percent", "amount": 10 }
  ],
  "variants": [
    { "price": null,            // null → inherit the product price
      "sale_price": null,
      "calculated_price": 79.99 }
  ]
}

The catalog stores inputs. calculated_price is the read-only output — never set it directly.

// PRICE LIST RECORD — a per-variant override for one currency
// PUT /v3/pricelists/{id}/records
{
  "variant_id": 3121,           // records target a VARIANT, never a product
  "price": 10.00,
  "sale_price": 8.00,
  "currency": "EUR",
  "calculated_price": 8.00      // read-only
}

// …and how it reaches an audience:
// POST /v3/pricelists/assignments
{ "price_list_id": 7, "customer_group_id": 2, "channel_id": 1 }

A record overrides one variant's price in one currency; an assignment points the whole list at a customer group and/or channel.

// BATCH PRICING API — resolve the stack for a specific shopper context
// POST /v3/pricing/products   body: { channel_id, currency_code, customer_group_id, items:[…] }
// one returned item — every price is a TAX object:
{
  "product_id": 111,
  "variant_id": 222,
  "price":            { "as_entered": 79.99, "entered_inclusive": false,
                        "tax_exclusive": 79.99, "tax_inclusive": 87.99 },
  "calculated_price": { "as_entered": 79.99, "entered_inclusive": false,
                        "tax_exclusive": 79.99, "tax_inclusive": 87.99 }
}

This is the endpoint that answers "what does this shopper pay for this config in this channel?" — the whole stack, resolved, with tax computed both ways.

Why an override stack

The layered model exists so one product can serve every pricing situation without duplicating the product. Sales, wholesale tiers, per-channel prices, quantity breaks, and per-currency lists are all just different layers of the same stack, and the fixed weight order makes the outcome deterministic: given a shopper, a channel, and a cart quantity, exactly one layer wins each decision, and calculated_price reports the result. This is also where Lesson 3's frozen list_price comes from — the line item captures whatever the stack resolved to at add-time, which is why a later sale or price-list change never rewrites an existing cart. The catalog computes; the line item remembers.

Key line "A BigCommerce price is not a field — it's the output of an ordered override stack: default → sale → variant → price list → customer-group discount / bulk → modifiers → tax, read top to bottom with tax last. calculated_price is the read-only resolved result, and price lists are variant-level overrides aimed at a customer group or channel."
The trap Two shallow readings. First: calculated_price is not "price minus a discount" — it is the resolved shopper price after sale prices and adjustment rules, a read-only output you never set. Second: the layers do not all stack. A price-list price overrides everything before it and switches off two later layers for that SKU — product bulk pricing is skipped, and customer-group discounts do not apply on top of price-list prices. Assuming "every discount adds up" will overstate the shopper's savings and misprice the cart.

Recall — answer out loud before peeking:

① Is calculated_price something you set, or something BigCommerce computes? What does it represent?

show answer

Computed — it's read-only. It's the resolved shopper price after the override stack runs (sale price + variant + price list + adjustment rules), not "price minus a discount."

② At what level does a price list set prices — product or variant — and what happens if it has no entry for a variant?

show answer

Variant level only (a price record targets a variant_id). If a variant isn't in the active price list, catalog pricing is used as the fallback.

③ A SKU has a price-list price. Do product bulk-pricing quantity breaks and customer-group discounts still apply to it?

show answer

No to both. Price-list prices exclude the SKU from product bulk pricing, and customer-group discounts do not apply on top of price-list prices.

④ Where in the stack does tax sit, and which field says whether the entered price already included it?

show answer

Tax is last, after shipping and promotions. entered_inclusive flags whether the as_entered price was tax-inclusive; the API also returns computed tax_inclusive and tax_exclusive.
