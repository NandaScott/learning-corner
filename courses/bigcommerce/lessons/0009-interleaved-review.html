Interleaved review

Re-reading feels like learning but mostly builds fluency — the illusion of mastery. Retrieving from memory, cold, with topics shuffled, is what builds storage strength.

How to use this

Questions are drawn from all eight lessons and shuffled every run, by design. Interleaving (mixing topics rather than reviewing one lesson at a time) is harder, and that difficulty is what makes it stick.1 Most questions pit the right answer against a near-miss: the shallow version that sounds right. Answer from memory before reading the feedback. Then hit Shuffle & retry and run it again in a few days — that spacing is the second half of durable memory.

Go deeper. Missed the same lesson twice? Flag it — it's worth re-explaining a different way, or drilling just that concept. Clearing this cold, two sessions apart, means you can trace a purchase from catalog to shipped order — carts, line items, pricing, discounts, consignments, and status — without notes.
